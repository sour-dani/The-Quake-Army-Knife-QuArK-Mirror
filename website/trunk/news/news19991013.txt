Of course, and so can Tiglari. The curved surfaces in Quake3 are quadratic
Bezier patches, meaning there are defined with 3x3 control points. In
QuArK you will see these control points and be able to move them; QuArK
draws the resulting interpolated patch as a wireframe grid on the map
views. Moving the control points around is theoretically enough to do
anything related to Bezier patches but in practive we will need a lot more
features, like cutting a patch in 2-3 parts, smoothing with adjacent
patches, and much, much more.<br>
<br>
Texture on Bezier patches is given by two texture coordinates for each
control point. This means that each of the 9 control points actually has 5
coordinates : x-y-z-s-t, where s and t are for the texture. These s-t
coordinates are interpolated between control points in the same way as the
spacial x-y-z coordinates. In QuArK, when you select a Bezier patch, you
will see the 3x3 control points drawn over a textured background in a new
page at left, after the Tree, Specs, Poly, Face and 3D pages. Moving these
"s-t" control points will distort the texture in the same way as moving
the "x-y-z" control points on the map view changes the shape of the Bezier
patch.<br>
<br>
Note that I talked about "interpolation between control points" but the
Bezier patch actually touches only the 4 corner control points; the other
ones are rather "attractors" that distort the shape in their direction.<br>
