BTW, I have also put Joe Water's "no fullbrights" PSP palette on the download page (along with the old one that included the fullbrights).<BR>
Haven't had anytime to work on my "showcase" level lately, but I did try out some new compiling tools. I downloaded Ken Alverson's new QBSP (which is much improved now that he got rid of that annoying POP error and changed it to a warning). I also tried out "Arghlite" by Tim Wright. It is a "light.exe" program that includes some new enhancements. The best features are that  you can specify the "fade distance" of your lights (from the command line), and the <B>best</B> thing is that simply by adding a "light" specific to worldspawn, you can now specify the minimum light setting for your level. This works excellent from QuArK. For instance setting <B>light 20</B> in worldspawn gives a faint light throughout your level.<BR>
I have replaced the "build.zip" file on the <A HREF="download.html">Download Page</A> with a zip file containing Ken Alverson's QBSP, Tim Wright's Arghlite, and RVIS. I have been having no problems with these versions, and they work well from Win95. (Included in the zip file is the original documentation for each program).
