<headline>Activision Blizzard is getting sued</headline>

<p>Several professional gamers are suing the maker of Call of Duty, Activision Blizzard. Why? Because, according to them, Activision Blizzard is <a href="https://www.reuters.com/legal/call-duty-gamers-sue-activision-monopolizing-leagues-tournaments-2024-02-16/">unlawfully monopolizing the Call of Duty leagues and tournaments</a>.</p>

<p>This isn't the first time Activision Blizzard has been sued for ruining competitive gaming. Just last year they had to settle a lawsuit in which they were accused of <a href="https://www.reuters.com/legal/us-files-antitrust-lawsuit-against-activision-blizzard-2023-04-03/">illegally suppressing gamers' wages in esports leagues</a>.</p>

<p>Now, don't worry, Activision Blizzard will survive just fine. I mean, they get sued all the time for even worse stuff, like <a href="https://www.techradar.com/news/activision-blizzard-lawsuit-a-timeline-of-key-events-and-everything-you-need-to-know">discrimination against female employees</a>. Or that time they got sued for <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/video-games/2022/03/04/activision-blizzard-employee-suicide-lawsuit/">driving an employee to suicide</a>. Or that time when Activision Blizzard <a href="https://www.ign.com/articles/2010/03/04/infinity-ward-founders-file-lawsuit-against-activision">refused to pay out royalty payments</a>.</p>

<p>Remember that time when video games used to be about video games, and not about "how much illegal stuff can we get away with"? <pic smiley_sad></p>
