<headline>Snapshot of QuArK 6.00 Beta</headline>
If you have been reading the <a href="http://www.egroups.com/group/quark">QuArK forum/mailing-list</a> more frequently than I,
you would know that Alex have made some snapshot-compilations of QuArK 6.00 Beta, which contains better bezier handling and incipient Soldier of Fortune support.<br>
So what is a <i>snapshot</i> anyway? Alex explains:
<blockquote>
[...] snapshots of the current <u>developement</u> version of QuArK can be downloaded at SourceForge.<br>
<br>
The files are named &nbsp;<code>quarksnapshot_&lt;datestamp&gt;.ace</code>&nbsp; and
contain a compile of QuArK sources at that date.<br>
<br>
<a href="http://sourceforge.net/project/filelist.php?group_id=1181">http://sourceforge.net/project/filelist.php?group_id=1181</a><br>
<br>
Be aware that these are <u>snapshots</u>.
That is not even an alpha release. The only thing that is sure for a snapshot, is that it could be successfully compiled.<br>
</blockquote>
Notice that these snapshot-archives are in <a href="http://www.winace.com">WinACE</a> format, not .zip!<br>
