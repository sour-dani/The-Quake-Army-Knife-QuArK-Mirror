<headline>HTML do not belong in e-mails!</headline>
<span class="sml">(Notice: Any e-mails I receive regarding this article will be ignored.)</span><br>
Some properly don't know it, but I firmly believes that <u>HTML does not belong in e-mails</u>.<br>
<i>"Whats this guy's problem?"</i> you may say. Well, what do you possibly get from using HTML in your e-mails, that you can't make in plain-text? I would say nothing special. Oh yeah, you can use different fonts, sizes and colors, but isn't that something which takes the reader away from the actual contents of the message?
Another problem with HTML in e-mails, is that they are up to 50% larger in size than plain-text, because of all the HTML-tags that is needed. Just look at the <i>source code</i> of your HTML e-mails, and you'll see.
On top of that, some might even post <u>both</u> HTML <u>and</u> plain-text in the same e-mail, which just makes it larger again.<br>
<i>"So what? 50% isn't that much, now is it!"</i> - Actually it is! <img src="pics/SmileySad.gif" align=absmiddle width=15 height=15> I live in a country, where we still have to Pay-Per-Minute we are online/using the phone.
It would be something like US' long-distance calls (I presume.) So imagine what would happen, if you had to download 50% more, per e-mail you receive when paying for the time you're online. The main parts of the e-mails don't contain anything that would compensate for the extra time it takes to download, now would it?!!<br>
<i>"Okay, but I <u>need</u> to make my text in underline/italic/bold, to clarify myself"</i> - Then why do you <u>need</u> to use HTML? What do you think they did, before HTML were invented? They used:
<blockquote><font face="Courier"><pre>_this text is underlined_
*This is written with bold fonts*
/And this line is written with italic-style/
AND HERE I SHOUT, WHICH YOU HOPEFULLY ALREADY KNOWS!
</pre></font></blockquote>
<i>"Ohh, shut up Decker! You're just old fashioned."</i> - Maybe I am, but think about it: How often do you <u>really</u> make use of the HTML capabilities in e-mails you write?
Another fact is also, that I, and hopefully many many others, are not using the all dominating MS Outlook Express. Its too "<i>Ohhh let me help you with that, you less-knowing user. I'm the program so <u><b>I</b></u> know whats best for you, my user!</i>" I truly hate programs which I can't customize, the way I want them to be, without going into a non-documented registry-database.<br>
<i>"But you <u>can</u> customize Outlook Express."</i> - Then why haven't <b>you</b> done it yet? Make it only send plain-text e-mails. <img src="pics/SmileyHappy.gif" align=absmiddle width=15 height=15><br>
<br>Okay, enough of my ranting... Move along... Move along, nothing more to see here...
