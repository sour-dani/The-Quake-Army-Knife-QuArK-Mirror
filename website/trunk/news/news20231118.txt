<headline>Half-life 1 turns 25 years!</headline>

<p>Half-life 1 turns 25 years old! For this special occasion, Valve has released <a href="https://half-life.com/en/halflife25">a new update</a> adding some much-needed quality-of-life features and fixes! There's better widescreen support, additional multiplayer maps, and more. There's also some added support for modders specifically, such as func_vehicle! Go check it out!</p>

<p>Valve also included this statement: "We now consider this anniversary version of Half Life to be the definitive version, and the one we'll continue to support going forward." I know some of you are still running on period-correct hardware and operating systems, so it's important to note that this statement means that the original retail versions of Half-life 1 are now officially and publicly recognized by Valve as abandonware, and if you want to play the game with these latest patches on older hardware (Half-life 1 on Steam demands at least Windows XP), you have to "find" a copy of the game with these patches for your PC and Valve doesn't accept money for this.</p>
