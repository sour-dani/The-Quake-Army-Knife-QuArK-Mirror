<headline>Tiglari speaks about 3Dfx</headline>
For those of you, who hasn't joined the <a href="forums.php">QuArK-forums</a>, and has problems with the new
Voodoo drivers, here is something that might interest you <span class="sml">(<a href="http://www.egroups.com/group2476.html?">original message</a>)</span>:
<blockquote>
[Tiglari]<br>
Someone on the hereticii.com msgboard has just reported that they got
the 3dfx view working with their Voodoo-2 <span class="sml">[incorrectly mentioned as Banshee]</span> by setting the screen size to
640x480, instead of its default, which is 560x420.  So it would be
really excellent if everyone with other problematic Voodoo setups tried
that out and reported what happens (I'll be doing Voodoo1).  The lower
default resolution is suppoosed to increase rendering speed and so is
there for a reason, but if we know that it's causing the problem that
will make it easier to find the right fix.<br>
<br>
Actually I don't at this point understand why this works, because for
Voodoo-1 the black death occurs without any input from this config option as
far as I can tell, but there could be several different problems
appearing with different cards (the SetIntelPrecision trick that worked
for me didn't work for decker, iirc).
</blockquote>
To change the 3Dfx screensize, you go into <a target="_blank" href="http://www.planetquake.comhelp/configuration/index.html#general3dview">QuArK's configuration-options, general, 3D-view</a>.<br>
A Banshee user, who tried the suggestion, reports:
<blockquote>
[James Woolley]<br>
I own a banshee and tried the 640x480 trick.. the only thing I got was
instead of the black screen I got actual rendered stuff.. except it was
frozen as soon at it came up.  So I got it to actualy show sumething but it
still froze
</blockquote>
And there's more about a possible solution of the 3Dfx/Voodoo problem.<br>

</blockquote>
