The screenshots were taken with GLQuake, and I noticed that they seem brighter than in WinQuake. I'll have to fiddle around with the lighting some more so it doesn't wreck the "atmosphere". BTW, I just started using Ken Alverson's new "WQBSP". (Regardless of the name, it will work for other editors too). I got it from <A HREF="http://www.bluesnews.com/" TARGET="_top">Blues News</A>, but you could probably pick it up off the PQ's own <A HREF="http://www.planetquake.com/worldcraft/">The Forge</A>. It supports transparent water, HIP's entities, and seems much less buggy than the others I've been using. It also supports multiple texture wads, but we all know that if you use a "certain editor" that that feature is totally unnecessary :-). It's *slightly* slower than Woofer's (Tom Grangent's (sp?)) build of QBSP, but it doesn't give me the bogus "point off plane" and "no entities in blah blah" errors that Woofer's was. All in all, I'd recommend it.
