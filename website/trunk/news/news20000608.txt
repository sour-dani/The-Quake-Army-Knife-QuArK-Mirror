It seems that in netscape somehow 0A (hex) gets expanded to 0D0A (hex)
at the download. So if the archive has a file length that is
longer then I wrote, that is a download problem. Use the
IE instead or any other browser.<br>
</blockquote>
I'm not quite sure what Alex is talking about here, other than when someone tries to view text-files in Netscape,
and then does a "Save as..." all LineFeed-characters (0A in hex) gets converted to CarriageReturn+LineFeed (0D0A in hex) and thus
mangles the text-file.<br>
So when you want to download something, remember always to hold down SHIFT and then click on the file link.<br>
<span class="sml">(Which I also noticed that Daniel O'Connor states, in his more technical explanation in the <a href="http://www.egroups.com/messages/quark">QuArK forum</a> - Thanks for the CC Daniel.)</span><br>
